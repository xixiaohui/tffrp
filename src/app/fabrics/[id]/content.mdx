# Glass Fiber Fabrics Technical Data Sheet (TDS)

Glass fiber fabrics are woven textile reinforcements made from E-glass continuous filaments. They offer high tensile strength, dimensional stability, chemical resistance, and thermal endurance, making them ideal for structural composites across aerospace, marine, automotive, and construction industries.

## Application Processes

- **Wet Lay-Up / Hand Lay-Up**  
- **Resin Transfer Molding (RTM)**  
- **Vacuum Infusion**  
- **Pultrusion (for prepreg-type unidirectionals)**  
- **Prepreg Laminating with hot-melt epoxy or polyester resins**  

## End-Use Applications

- **Marine**: boat hulls, decks, masts  
- **Aerospace**: radomes, fairings, lightweight panels  
- **Automotive**: hoods, bumpers, structural parts  
- **Wind Energy**: turbine blades, nacelles  
- **Construction**: FRP panels, façade systems  
- **Electrical**: PCB prepregs, cable insulation layers  
- **Industrial**: FRP tanks, pipes, gratings  

## Storage & Handling

- Store in **cool, dry, and shaded** environments  
- Keep away from **moisture, chemical fumes, and direct sunlight**  
- Recommended temperature: **15–30°C**; Relative Humidity: < 65%  
- **Shelf life**: 12 months under proper storage conditions  
- Handle rolls with care to avoid **edge fraying** or fabric distortion

## Packaging

- Roll Widths: 1000 mm, 1270 mm, 1550 mm (custom available)  
- Core: Paper or PVC inner tube (76 mm ID)  
- Packaging: **PE film wrap + cardboard box or pallet**  
- Labels include: **Product code, batch number, net weight, production date**

## Quality Control

All batches of fiberglass fabric are rigorously tested:

- Area weight and weave uniformity  
- Yarn tension and warp/weft ratio  
- Sizing compatibility with customer resin system  
- Dimensional stability and shrinkage under cure  
- Flame resistance and dielectric breakdown voltage (if required)
