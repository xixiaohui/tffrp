# Glass Fiber Roving Technical Data Sheet (TDS)

Glass fiber roving is a bundle of parallel continuous E-glass filaments, designed for reinforcement applications in composites. It offers high tensile strength, corrosion resistance, and excellent compatibility with a variety of resin systems.

## Features & Advantages

- **High tensile strength and stiffness** for structural reinforcement
- **Low fuzz and excellent unwinding** performance for high-speed processing
- **Good dispersion** and resin wet-out for superior mechanical bonding
- **Corrosion and moisture resistant**, ideal for harsh environments
- **Thermal and electrical insulation** properties make it suitable for demanding composite systems
- **Low LOI for clean processing** in thermosetting resins

## Application Processes

Glass fiber rovings are suitable for the following processes:

- **Pultrusion** – for profiles, rods, rebar
- **Filament Winding** – for tanks, pipes, pressure vessels
- **RTM (Resin Transfer Molding)** – for automotive and electrical components
- **Spray-up and Hand Lay-up** – for marine, construction
- **Weaving / Knitting** – to manufacture woven roving and multiaxial fabrics
- **Sheet Molding Compound (SMC)** – when chopped into strands

## Typical Applications

- Wind turbine blades  
- GRP pipes and pressure vessels  
- Electrical cable cores and insulation  
- Automotive leaf springs, hoods, and panels  
- Building reinforcement bars (GFRP rebar)  
- FRP bridge components, ladders, gratings

## Storage & Handling

- Store in **dry and well-ventilated** areas, temperature 15–35°C, RH < 65%
- Avoid **direct sunlight, moisture, and mechanical stress**
- Keep in **original packaging** before use
- Recommended shelf life: **12 months** from production date

## Quality Assurance

All roving products undergo full in-process and final quality checks:

- Tex verification and strand uniformity  
- Moisture content and LOI testing  
- Strand tension uniformity during unwinding  
- Compatibility test with target resin system
