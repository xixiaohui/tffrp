# Glass Fiber Mats Technical Data Sheet (TDS)

Glass fiber mats are non-woven reinforcement materials composed of randomly oriented chopped strands or continuous filaments. They are primarily used in FRP (fiber-reinforced plastic) processes, including hand lay-up, pultrusion, and compression molding, offering excellent resin compatibility, formability, and mechanical reinforcement.

## Features & Advantages

- **Uniform fiber distribution** for consistent mechanical properties  
- **Fast wet-out and good resin penetration** for increased production efficiency  
- **High tensile strength and dimensional stability**  
- **Excellent formability** around complex contours  
- **Low fuzz and dust** for cleaner working conditions  
- **Chemical resistance** to acids, alkalis, and organic solvents (especially for C-glass mats)

## Typical Applications

- Hand lay-up and spray-up FRP manufacturing  
- Boat hulls, water tanks, bathware, automotive panels  
- Pultrusion profiles (SBM, Combo Mat)  
- Pipe wrap and laminated sheet production  
- Molded parts in construction, marine, and transportation industries  
- Electrical insulation in transformers and switchgears (CFM)

## Handling & Storage

- Store in a **dry, clean, and well-ventilated** warehouse  
- Keep away from **direct sunlight and moisture**  
- Recommended storage temperature: **15–35°C**, humidity < 65%  
- Rolls should be kept **vertically** or laid flat with no stacking over 2 layers

## Quality Control

All mats are subjected to strict quality inspection including:

- Area weight and thickness verification  
- Tensile and peel strength testing  
- Binder and moisture content analysis  
- Visual inspection for uniformity, defects, and contamination
